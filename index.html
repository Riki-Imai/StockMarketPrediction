Outline

Introduction:

Predicting the stock markets movements are of great interest for hedge fund, quants fund and also investors. Financial Securities firms try to develop algorithms that automatically trades with information from news articles, data, and market trends. Some hedge/quants funds experience an increase in their markets, and computational analysis is developing rapidly. (Note that, it is nearly impossible to deal with outliers with algorithms or neural networks, known as "Black Swan" market theory. Also as presented in "random walk theory," it is impossible to predict the precise price movement with a huge amount of noises.)

Background:

Automating the Prediction of stock market fluctuations has been a difficult matter to a lot of companies trying to do so, mainly because of the amount of factors that can affect stock changes and make predicting them perfectly nearly impossible. For example, Eli Lily’s stock dived down after a fake twitter account impersonating them tweeted with the promise of free insulin. Such events create the difficulty of predicting daily stock changes.

Transition:

The main issue with predicting the stock market is that it does not always follow quantitative data, and changes seem illogical a lot of times. The problem with this is there is not a publicly available solution to this issue, and hedge funds cannot reveal their strategies to deal with this seemingly-illogical behavior as it is their method of making money. In this project, we will research more about this behavior and try to increase our accuracy by attempting to include this behavior in our model.

Details:

One of the technical challenges that we will have to overcome is finding an accurate dataset as our prediction model heavily depends on the completeness of the dataset. Finding a perfect dataset will be difficult but crucial to the project.

Assessment:

Our main goal is to achieve a working model while maximizing the accuracy as much as we can. If time allows, we would like to try our model on actual stock markets with this website (https://www.marketwatch.com/games).

Ethical Sweep:

General Questions:

  • Should we even be doing this?
    • It will be useful to see how the company might grow and analyze what the audience thinks about the company, because stock markets are where all the opinions and information will merge and be reflected.
  • What might be the accuracy of a simple non-ML alternative?
    • 50%
  • What processes will we use to handle appeals/mistakes?
    • By nature, there are a lot of imperfections with trying to predict the stock market (“random walk theory”). However, we will try to get the most accurate average prediction line we can get.
  • How diverse is our team?
    • Everyone comes from the different background and interests.

Data Questions:

  • Is our data valid for its intended use?
    • There is the Public Datasets (PDS) that has actual markets data, such as JPX and Deutsche Börse Public Dataset, which is publicly accessible and can be used in the research as seen in https://www.originate.com/thinking/predicting-stock-market-movements-using-a-neural-network
  • What bias could be in our data? (All data contains bias.)
    • Not biased with the data itself, but data itself contains “bias”, but does not seem impactful in nature; the stock price moves with selling/buying the stocks, which are done by humans with bias.
  • How could we minimize bias in our data and model?
    • We do not do anything to minimize bias in our data since the stock market already reflects these in themselves.
  • How should we “audit” our code and data?
    • By setting code reviews after each major progress point for the project.

Impact Questions:

  • Do we expect different error rates for different sub-groups in the data?
    • We expect there is a variety of movement patterns depending on the industry and companies’ stocks that we will look for in nature. For this reason, we will first try to focus on a handful of companies in a single industry and expand the number possibly.
  • What are likely misinterpretations of the results and what can be done to prevent those misinterpretations?
    • Stock market data contains noises. By this nature, we might misinterpret the randomness of data as “predicting correctly.” To avoid this, we will mention how financial markets can be random sometimes by sharing some theories established.
  • How might we impinge individuals' privacy and/or anonymity?
    • The stock market prices do not reflect anyone’s privacy and are kept anonymous since there is no way to tell who makes the trading from quantitative stock market data.

Related Works Search

  • Predicting Stock Market Movements Using A Neural Network Applied To The Deutsche Börse Public Dataset
    • Using a neural network onto the Public Dataset (PDS), Deutsche Börse Public Dataset, they implemented a neural network model to predict future movements of stock prices using trends from the previous 10 minutes. Although the dataset for stock markets is provided on a minute-to-minute basis, it usually contains a lot of noises in data in order to predict the trend; they averaged 10 1-minute data into a single datapoint in order to avoid these noises.
  • Investopedia: Neural Networks: Forecasting Profits
    • This article summarizes the backgrounds and information about using a neural network to predict the price movements. Because of stock market features, adjustment and retraining of models is essential regularly to incorporate the recent trend and effects of new policies, any publicly available information to maintain the model to be effective.
  • Short-term stock market price trend prediction using a comprehensive deep learning system
    • They proposed a comprehensive customization of feature engineering and deep learning-based models for predicting price trends of stock markets, including pre-processing of the stock market dataset. This paper serves to enhance understanding of attempts to predict stock markets and also contribute to the stock analysis research both in finance and tech domains.
  • Stock market value prediction using neural networks
    • This article experiments with two ways of predicting stock market prices. First way is through a feed-forward MLP (Multi-layer Perceptron) and an Elman recurrent network, with a back-propagation algorithm being used to train those networks. This article finds that the feed-forward MLP network more accurately predicts the direction of change and also the amount of change.
  • Bayesian neural networks for stock price forecasting before and during COVID-19 pandemic
    • This article utilizes Bayesian neural networks to predict the stock market prices during high volatility (utilizing data gathered during covid pandemic). They find that this type of neural network provides not only reasonable predictions of stock market prices, but also find the neural network provides good quantification of the uncertainty of the predictions.
  • Stock Closing Price Prediction using Machine Learning Techniques
    • This article uses neural networks and random forest techniques to predict future closing prices for stocks. The method was used on five companies from different markets. The standard strategic indicators RMSE and MAPE were used to evaluate the mode.
  • YouTube: Predict the Stock Market with Machine Learning and Python
    • This video is a tutorial on using Python to predict the S&P 500 prices. They also address common pitfalls in overfitting data.
  • Application of Artificial Neural Network for stock market predictions: A review of literature
    • Neural networks can be used for stock price prediction: The article proposes the use of artificial neural networks (ANNs) to predict stock prices, as ANNs are able to recognize patterns and trends in data, and can be trained to make accurate predictions based on past performance. Different architectures and parameters can affect prediction accuracy: The article notes that the accuracy of ANNs in predicting stock prices is affected by various factors such as the architecture of the network, the number of hidden layers and nodes, and the activation function used. Accuracy can be improved by incorporating more data: The article suggests that incorporating more data such as news and social media sentiment, economic indicators, and company financial reports can improve the accuracy of stock price predictions, and highlights the need for ongoing research in this area.
  • Stock Prediction Using Artificial Neural Networks
    • Different ANN models have different prediction accuracies: The article compares the performance of different ANN models on stock price prediction and finds that certain models, such as the backpropagation neural network (BPNN), are better at predicting stock prices than others. Accurate prediction depends on careful selection of input variables: The article notes that selecting the appropriate input variables, such as stock prices, trading volumes, and economic indicators, is crucial for accurate stock price prediction using ANNs, and suggests that combining multiple models and input variables can lead to more accurate predictions.
